1. Summary

In short:

  • CoffeeCheck lets you rate and remember espressos at places you visit.
  • You sign in with Apple or Google. We store a minimal account (an identifier, your email and display name).
  • Your ratings, private notes and private photos are yours; photos are stored privately in the European Union.
  • Your approximate location is used only to show nearby places while you search — it is not stored on our servers.
  • We do not sell your data, show ads, or use third-party advertising or tracking.
  • You can delete your account and all associated data at any time from within the App.

2. Information we collect

Account information

To use features that require an account you sign in with Sign in with Apple or Google Sign-In. When you do, we receive and store:

  • a stable user identifier provided by Apple or Google;
  • your email address;
  • your display name (if provided);
  • which provider you used and the dates you created the account and last signed in.

Apple only shares your name and email the first time you sign in. If you choose Apple’s “Hide My Email” feature, we receive a relay address instead of your real email.

Your coffee content

  • Ratings — for each place you rate: the coffee type (single/double espresso), the size and taste ratings, and whether water was served.
  • Private notes — optional free-text notes you write for yourself.
  • Private photos — optional photos you attach to a rating (see section 4).
  • Favorites — places you mark as favorites.
  • Place identifiers — the Google Place ID and basic details (name, address, type) of the places you rate or favorite.

Location information

With your permission, the App uses your device’s approximate location to prioritise nearby results when you search. See section 5 for details.

Technical & diagnostic information

Our backend records limited technical and diagnostic data (such as error logs, request metadata and timestamps) to keep the service reliable and secure. This may include your device’s IP address in server logs. We do not use this data to build advertising or marketing profiles.

4. Photos

Photos you attach to a rating are private to you. Before upload, each photo is resized and compressed on your device, then stored in a private cloud storage container hosted in the European Union. Photos are only accessible to your signed-in account through short-lived, access-controlled links; they are not made public and are not shared with other users. Deleting a photo, deleting the rating, or deleting your account removes the photo from our storage.

5. Location

If you grant location permission (“While Using the App”), we use your device’s approximate location only to bias search results toward places near you. This location is sent, together with your search term, to Google’s Places service to return relevant nearby results. We do not store your location on our servers, and we do not track your movements. You can turn location access off at any time in your device settings; search will then work without a “near me” bias.

6. How your information is shared

We do not sell your personal data. We share data only with the service providers needed to run the App:

  • Google — place search, place details and place photos are provided by the Google Places API, and Google Sign-In is used for authentication. When you search, your search term and (if available) your approximate location are sent to Google to return results. See the Google Privacy Policy.
  • Apple — Sign in with Apple is used for authentication. See the Apple Privacy Policy.
  • Microsoft Azure — our backend, database and photo storage are hosted on Microsoft Azure in the European Union (West Europe region), acting as our data processor. See the Microsoft Privacy Statement.

We may also disclose data where required by law, or to protect our rights, safety, or the integrity of the service.

7. Where your data is stored & security

Your account data, ratings, notes and photos are stored on Microsoft Azure in the European Union (West Europe). We use industry-standard measures to protect your data, including encryption in transit (HTTPS), private (non-public) photo storage with short-lived access links, and authentication using signed session tokens. Some data (such as favorites) may also be cached on your device so the App works quickly and offline; this local data is removed when you sign out or delete your account.

8. Data retention

We keep your account data and content for as long as your account exists. You can remove individual ratings, notes or photos at any time, and you can delete your entire account (see section 10), which erases your data from our systems. Limited technical logs are retained for a short period for security and troubleshooting and are then deleted or anonymised.

9. Your rights

Under the GDPR you have the right to:

  • access the personal data we hold about you;
  • have inaccurate data corrected;
  • have your data erased (“right to be forgotten”);
  • restrict or object to certain processing;
  • receive your data in a portable format;
  • withdraw consent at any time (e.g. by turning off location or removing photos);
  • lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority — in the Netherlands, the Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us using the details in section 14.

10. Deleting your account

You can delete your account at any time from within the App: Profile → Delete account. This permanently deletes your account record, ratings, notes, favorites and photos from our systems, and revokes the sign-in grant associated with your account. Locally cached data on your device is also removed. This action cannot be undone.

11. Children

CoffeeCheck is not directed at children and is not intended for use by anyone under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us and we will delete it.

12. International transfers

We store your data in the European Union. Some of our providers (such as Google and Apple) may process certain data (for example authentication or place-search requests) outside the EU. Where that happens, those providers rely on appropriate safeguards such as the EU Standard Contractual Clauses, as described in their own privacy policies linked above.

13. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we make material changes, we will update the “Last updated” date at the top of this page. Your continued use of the App after an update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
